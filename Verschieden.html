V E R S C H I E D E N

Die Welt ist voller verschiedener Dinge. Es gibt verschiedene Arten von Pflanzen und Tiere, verschiedene Arten von Menschen mit unterschiedlichen Hautfarben, Kulturen und Sprachen. Selbst innerhalb einer bestimmten Gruppe gibt es Unterschiede, wie zum Beispiel verschiedene Geschmacksrichtungen von Lebensmitteln oder verschiedene Musikstile. Diese Vielfalt der verschiedenen Dinge ist es, was die Welt interessant und lebendig macht. Es gibt immer etwas Neues zu entdecken und zu lernen, und es hilft uns, die Welt aus verschiedenen Perspektiven zu betrachten. Es ist wichtig, die Unterschiede zu akzeptieren und zu schätzen, anstatt sie zu verurteilen oder zu ignorieren, denn jeder hat das Recht auf Selbstverwirklichung und seine eigene Identität.

2020-2021

Schools in Indonesia were closed for 2 weeks in March 2020 to reduce the spread of the COVID-19 virus. This policy was taken after the number of detected cases significantly increased. The main goal of closing schools was to prevent the spread of the virus among students and teachers, and to protect the general public's health.

During the closure period, schools in Indonesia implemented remote learning (online learning) so that students could continue to learn and follow lessons. However, there are still a number of students who have difficulty following remote learning due to limited internet access or the equipment needed.

The government also provided financial assistance to students and teachers who were facing economic difficulties during the school closure period. However, there are still a number of students who are facing difficulties in following remote learning due to limited internet access or the equipment needed.

COVID-19

In addition, the government also took other actions such as providing health facilities and strengthening the health care system throughout Indonesia to deal with the pandemic. However, there are still some problems faced by the community such as economic difficulties and mental health problems caused by the pandemic.

Overall, the closure of schools for 2 weeks in Indonesia is expected to help reduce the spread of the COVID-19 virus, however after two weeks schools are still closed indefinitely and further action is still needed to address the problems caused by this pandemic.
